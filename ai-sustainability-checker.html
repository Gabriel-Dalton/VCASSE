Evidence-backed context (why this matters)

Grid impact is accelerating

IEA reports data centres used around 415 TWh in 2024 (~1.5% of world electricity) and projects ~945 TWh by 2030 in its base case.[1]

U.S. growth is steep

DOE/FEMP notes U.S. data centres consumed 176 TWh in 2023 (about 4.4% of U.S. electricity) with projected 2023–2028 CAGR of 13% to 27%.[2]

Water is a core issue

Microsoft reports contracting 50,031,857 m³ water replenishment in FY24 and cites a Quincy project cutting local potable use by 97%.[3]

Methodology (transparent assumptions)

  • Prompt-level baselines are calibrated to publicly disclosed median text-prompt figures from Google research (0.24 Wh, 0.03 g CO₂e, and 0.26 mL water per prompt under their measured conditions).[4]
  • Profiles (Light/Balanced/Heavy) scale these baseline intensities by workflow complexity and token volume for educational comparisons, not audited accounting.
  • System-wide context (IEA/DOE/corporate sustainability reports) is presented so users can connect personal usage to infrastructure realities.[1][2][5]
