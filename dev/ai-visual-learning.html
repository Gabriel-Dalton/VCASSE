VISUAL AI LEARNING HUB

AI concepts made visual, local, and practical.

Explore animated explainers for LLMs, neural networks, CNNs, and key safety concepts—with Vancouver-focused examples and source footnotes.

Large Language Models (LLMs)[1]

LLMs predict the next token in a sequence. They can summarize, translate, and assist with drafting by learning statistical language patterns from large datasets.

Vancouver example: A multilingual city-service assistant can summarize transit notices across English, Punjabi, and Mandarin.

Neural Networks[2]

Neural networks pass inputs through layers of weighted connections. During training, those weights are adjusted to reduce prediction error.

Vancouver example: Routing housing support requests to the right service channel based on urgency and needs.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)[3]

CNNs use learnable filters to detect visual patterns like edges, textures, and objects. They are commonly used for image classification tasks.

Vancouver example: Classifying shoreline photos to identify marine debris patterns over time.

Attention Mechanisms[4]

Attention lets a model focus on the most relevant tokens when generating each next token, improving long-range reasoning and context handling.

Vancouver example: Question-answering over local policy documents, where key clauses need weighted focus.

Embeddings[5]

Embeddings turn words or items into vectors so semantic similarity can be measured mathematically and searched efficiently.

Vancouver example: Matching residents to training programs using meaning-based similarity, not only keyword matches.

AI Safety, Bias, and Sustainability[6]

Responsible AI requires model safety checks, fairness audits, and clear awareness of environmental costs such as compute energy use.

Vancouver example: Evaluating whether public-facing AI tools are accurate across neighborhoods and language groups.
