A Sustainability Checklist for AI Procurement

How organizations can ask better vendor questions around energy use, lifecycle impact, and reporting transparency.

  • PublishedFeb 27, 2026
  • Reading6 min
  • AuthorsDr. Alejandro Ruiz, Linh Nguyen

Many organizations buy AI capabilities without visibility into compute intensity, infrastructure sourcing, or lifecycle environmental costs. The contract is signed, the API key is provisioned, and only later does someone ask whether the energy footprint matches the institution's climate commitments.

Four questions every contract should answer

  1. Workload energy ranges. What is a typical kWh-per-1000-requests for the workload class we will use?
  2. Region and grid mix. Where will inference run, and what is the grid intensity at that location during our hours?
  3. Water and cooling. Is potable water used for cooling? What is the water usage effectiveness for the relevant facility?
  4. Lifecycle and end-of-life. How is hardware retired, and is e-waste handled by a certified processor?

Make sustainability a contract clause, not a hope

Verbal commitments don't survive procurement renewals. We recommend a short standardized clause that requires annual environmental disclosures and gives the buyer the right to publish them. If a vendor cannot answer the four questions above, that is a finding worth recording before signing.

Procurement is climate policy

Cities and public institutions buy enormous quantities of AI capacity. The questions they ask shape the market more than any non-binding pledge. Vancouver-area institutions are well-positioned to lead by treating procurement as the climate lever it is.

"You don't need a perfect emissions number. You need a number you can compare next year."

The four-question checklist

  • Workload energy ranges per 1k requests.
  • Inference region and grid mix.
  • Water and cooling intensity.
  • Hardware lifecycle and e-waste handling.
