Energy Footprint Reporting: A Starter Standard

A baseline reporting format for teams that want to disclose AI energy impact with minimal overhead.

  • PublishedNov 19, 2025
  • Reading6 min
  • AuthorsDr. Alejandro Ruiz

Energy reporting in AI is currently inconsistent. One team reports per-token energy, another reports per-request, a third reports nothing at all. Comparing across institutions is nearly impossible, which means accountability is too.

Five fields, one quarterly cadence

This starter standard recommends reporting five fields, every quarter:

  • Workload class — e.g. "chat assistant", "document classification", "image generation".
  • Region — the data centre region where most inference occurred.
  • Model family — the family or generation, not the exact checkpoint.
  • Energy estimate — kWh per million requests, with a confidence band.
  • Method — measured, modelled, or vendor-supplied.

That's it. Five fields. Quarterly. The simplicity is the point: a standard that is too heavy will be ignored, and a standard that is ignored disclosing nothing.

Confidence bands matter

Estimation in AI energy reporting is unavoidable. We strongly recommend publishing a confidence band rather than a single number. A range of "120 – 180 kWh/Mreq, modelled" is more honest, and more useful, than a precise-looking but unsupported figure.

How this fits with broader climate disclosure

The starter standard is designed to nest inside existing climate disclosure frameworks (GHG Protocol, ISO 14064, CDP). It does not replace them — it gives small teams a way to start producing comparable AI-specific numbers immediately, while larger teams build out full Scope 2 and Scope 3 accounting.

The five fields

  • Workload class.
  • Region.
  • Model family.
  • kWh per million requests, with confidence band.
  • Method — measured, modelled, or vendor-supplied.
